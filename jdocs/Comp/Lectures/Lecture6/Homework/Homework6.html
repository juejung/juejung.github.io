Exercise 1

The aim is to fill two vector arrays xv and yv with x and f(x) values, respectively, where

\[ f(x) = \frac{1}{\sigma \sqrt{2 \pi}} e^{- \frac{1}{2} \left( \frac{x- \mu}{\sigma} \right) ^2} \]

is the density of the standard normal distribution, that is a normal density with mean \( \mu=0 \) and standard deviation \( \sigma = 1 \). Let the xv values be uniformly spaced in \( xv = [-4, 4] \) with a small enough grid so that the function plot is not too bumpy. A gridspace of \( 0.01 \) is probably sufficient. Write a loop to fill in the values for \( xv \) and \( yv=f(xv) \). Then plot(xv,yv)
